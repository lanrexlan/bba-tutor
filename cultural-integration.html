Culture in Every Lesson

Cultural Integration Handbook

Celebrate Nigerian heritage through language, stories, music, art, and lived experiences. This handbook offers practical tools and examples to weave authentic cultural perspectives into rigorous, global curricula—respectfully and joyfully.

Why It Matters

Cultural integration strengthens identity, deepens engagement, and connects learning to real life. When students see their cultures honoured, they participate more, retain better, and transfer knowledge across contexts.

Language as Asset

Use Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa and other Nigerian languages phrases to scaffold understanding and build pride, while maintaining academic English mastery.

Stories & Proverbs

Teach big ideas through folktales and sayings, linking moral reasoning to modern dilemmas.

Arts & Rituals

Explore music, textiles, festivals, and crafts to animate concepts in science, maths, and humanities.

Story Bank

Folktales to animate concepts. Use respectfully; avoid stereotyping and ensure accuracy.

The trickster tortoise, through greed, falls and cracks his shell. Use to discuss decisions and consequences.

  • Science: Materials—why shells crack; energy of falling.
  • English: Narrative structure; character motives.
  • PSHE: Honesty and community trust.

Legend of Moremi’s bravery in protecting her people. Discuss sacrifice, leadership, and civic duty.

  • History: Oral histories vs written sources.
  • English: Biography writing; persuasive speeches.
  • Art: Symbolism in statues and memorials.

Origin story linking to the Hausa city-states. Compare myths and migration narratives.

  • Geography: Water sources and settlement.
  • English: Compare origin myths across regions.
  • Citizenship: Founding stories and identity.

Encourage learners to verify versions with family elders or community sources where appropriate.

Respect & Safeguarding

A do-no-harm checklist for culturally responsive practice.

Consent & Context: Obtain permission before sharing personal stories or photos; explain purpose and audience.
Avoid Stereotypes: Present diverse voices; avoid portraying cultures as static or monolithic.
Sacred Knowledge: Skip restricted rituals or sacred items; focus on publicly available knowledge.
Language Respect: Acknowledge regional variants; avoid shaming learners for accents or code-switching.
Source Quality: Cross-check with reputable sources; teach learners to evaluate claims critically.
Accessibility: Provide multiple representations—text, visuals, and hands-on options.
