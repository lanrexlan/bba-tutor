🎯 High-Impact Teaching

Teaching Best Practices

Practical, research-driven strategies for planning, delivery, assessment, and classroom culture. Adapt these tools to your context—anchored in African heritage, aimed at global excellence.

🏛️ Pillars

Pedagogical Pillars

The core ingredients of high-impact lessons.

🔍

Clarity

State learning objectives, success criteria, and models of excellence. Reference them throughout the lesson.

🙋

Active Engagement

Think–Pair–Share, mini whiteboards, cold-calling with warmth, and student explanations drive learning.

💬

Feedback

Frequent, specific, actionable. Use exit tickets and exemplars; plan time for revision and improvement.

📈

Rigour & Support

High expectations with scaffolds: sentence stems, visuals, guided practice, and structured talk.

♿

Inclusion

Differentiate content, process, and product; design accessible materials and multiple ways to show learning.

🌍

Relevance

Connect concepts to lives, communities, and Nigerian/African contexts for deeper meaning.

📖 Playbook

Best Practices Playbook

Start with outcomes, then design backwards.

Objectives & Criteria

  • SWBAT statements in student-friendly language
  • Success criteria and exemplars
  • Pre-plan checks for understanding

Sequencing

  • Hook → Input → Practice → Show → Reflect
  • Chunking and pacing
  • Homework that extends thinking

Cultural Links

  • Local examples, proverbs, case studies
  • Bilingual supports where helpful
  • Community resources/guest shares

Keep explanations crisp; maximise student thinking time.

Modelling

  • Think alouds for complex tasks
  • Dual coding: words + visuals
  • Worked examples → faded guidance

Talk Routines

  • Think–Pair–Share; Rally Coach
  • Cold call with warmth & wait time
  • Academic sentence stems

Checks for Understanding

  • Mini whiteboards or polls
  • Stop–Solve–Share cycles
  • Error analysis & reteach

Assess for learning, not just of learning.

Formative

  • Exit tickets; hinge questions
  • Peer assessment with rubrics
  • Misconception probes

Summative

  • Performance tasks & practicals
  • Projects with public audience
  • Moderation for fairness

Feedback

  • WWW/EBI: What Went Well / Even Better If
  • Actionable next steps
  • Dedicated improvement time

Relationships first: respect, safety, and joy.

Routines

  • Do Now; materials check
  • Signals & transitions
  • End-of-lesson reflection

Belonging

  • Pronounce names correctly
  • Celebrate effort and growth
  • Use student voice & choice

Safeguarding

  • Clear boundaries online
  • Report concerns promptly
  • Privacy & consent norms

📐 Differentiation Matrix

Adjust the challenge, not the destination. Offer multiple pathways to the same goals.

Content

  • Texts at varied reading levels
  • Audio/visual supports
  • Vocabulary pre-teaching

Process

  • Graphic organisers
  • Choice of strategies (TPR, visuals)
  • Guided vs. independent practice

Product

  • Essay, presentation, or model
  • Rubric with tiered criteria
  • Oral alternatives for some tasks

Environment

  • Quiet spaces vs. collaboration zones
  • Assistive tech and tools
  • Flexible timing where appropriate
