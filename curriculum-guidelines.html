📘 Academic Framework

Curriculum Guidelines

A clear, culturally grounded roadmap for teaching excellence across subjects and age groups. Use this guide to plan rigorous, engaging lessons that celebrate Nigerian heritage while meeting global standards.

📋 Purpose & Scope

Purpose & Scope

These guidelines outline the philosophy, scope-and-sequence, assessment expectations, and practical tools for lesson design across the Baobab Online Academy. Tutors are encouraged to adapt with professional judgement, ensuring learning is inclusive, inquiry-driven, and connected to authentic African contexts.

Guiding Principles

The Baobab approach blends rigor, relevance, and cultural resonance.

Assessment & Reporting

Balance formative feedback with rigorous summative evaluations.

🔍

Diagnostic

Starting Point

~10%
📝

Formative

During Learning

Low-stakes checks: exit tickets, think-pair-share, journals, mini-quizzes.

~30%
🏆

Summative

Mastery Evidence

Performance tasks, projects, practicals, and cumulative exams.

~50%
🤝

Engagement

~10%

✅ Provide rubric-aligned feedback within 5–7 days of major submissions.

❓ FAQs

Common Questions

How do I adapt pacing for mixed-ability groups?+
Use diagnostics in week 1 to identify readiness levels. Create core tasks for all, scaffolded supports for learners needing more time, and extension challenges for advanced learners. Record the grouping plan in your lesson template and rotate supports weekly.
What if technology limits certain activities?+
Provide low-tech alternatives (printables, screenshots, verbal explanations) and leverage offline tasks like reading, sketching, or data collection. Note the adaptation in your reflection for continuous improvement.
How much cultural content is expected per unit?+
Aim for at least two meaningful integrations per unit—e.g., a local case study plus a language, proverb, or artifact analysis—ensuring authenticity and curricular relevance.
How are assessments moderated?+
Teams sample student work against shared rubrics at mid-unit and end-of-unit checkpoints. Calibration discussions ensure shared standards and fair grading practices.
